##🌈 Pinky Learns Quantum

A new modality of learning quantum computing — free, adaptive, and powered by LLMs.

Welcome to Pinky Learns Quantum, an evolving, AI‑augmented learning journey into quantum physics and quantum computing. This repository is intentionally lightweight and directionally correct — a scaffolding that will grow as I learn, explore, and commit insights along the way.

The goal is simple:

    Build a self-contained, free, modular path into quantum computing — without requiring expensive textbooks or formal prerequisites.

This repo is structured around four pillars and a fixed 0–7 module horizon, allowing the “Pinky Quantum Path” to shape itself naturally over time.
🧱 Four Pillars of the Pinky Quantum Path
Pillar 1 — MIT Undergraduate Quantum Physics (Tier 0)

A structured exploration of MIT OCW Quantum Physics I–III lecture notes.
No code, no simulations — just physics intuition + LLM prompting.
Pillar 2 — Math Foundations

The minimal math quantum computing actually uses:
complex numbers, linear algebra, Dirac notation, tensor products, probability.
Pillar 3 — Quantum Computing Core

Qubits, gates, circuits, entanglement, measurement — the computational backbone.
Pillar 4 — Quantum Algorithms

Grover, Shor, VQE, QAOA, QML — the algorithms that matter.
🧭 Fixed Learning Horizon (Modules 0–7)

These modules define the stable “learning horizon” for the Pinky Quantum Path.
Everything in the four pillars feeds into these modules.
Module	Focus
0	Intuition, no math — videos, games, metaphors
1	Math foundations — complex numbers, linear algebra
2	Quantum mechanics for computing — wavefunctions, operators
3	Qubits, gates, circuits — the core of QC
4	Quantum algorithms — Grover, Shor, VQE, QAOA
5	Applications — chemistry, finance, optimization
6	Careers — roles, skills, pathways
7	Quantum news digest — staying updated
